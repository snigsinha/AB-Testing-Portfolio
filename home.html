A/B Testing: Cacti E-Commerce Page

Introduction

For my UI/UX class at Brown, I learned about A/B testing and how it's used to make incremental design decisions to optimise for an ideal outcome. For this assignment, we were asked to make two versions of the same cacti e-commerce browsing page with one small design difference.

Conclusion

The experiment found that neither interface A nor lead to shorter time to completion or higher return rate. This finding is limited by the small sample size of 12 users. Many of the users were also known to be classmates who were using the site as a way to inflate sample size. Instead, users for testing should ideally be using the site with the intention of potentially buying the cacti.

Overall, further testing needed to validate results. In further tests, having a larger sample size that includes a variety of users will be essential to producing accurate results with valuable findings.
